Bryan Pearson II

Welcome to my webpage! I am currently working on my Ph.D in Computer Science at the University of Central Florida. I joined the Department of Computer Science in Fall 2018. My advisor is Dr. Xinwen Fu. I am primarily interested in IoT security and forensics.

Prior to UCF, I received my Bachelor of Science from Stetson University in May 2018. My advisor was Dr. Daniel Plante.

You can check out some of my current and former research projects, my CV, and contact information through this webpage.

Greenifier

Greenifier

This project plots ambient particulate matter, temperature, and humidity data from sensors onto a map. Our current deployment of devices are built using the ESP32 microcontroller. We use AWS as a backend.

Seismic

Seismic

Seismic, or Security IoT Solution for Microcontrollers, implements state-of-the-art security features into low-cost IoT devices. Our case study is the ESP32 MCU along with the ATECC608A crypto processor. The source code is publicly available and can be found below.
